Note from the Chairman

WHAT WE STAND FOR

WE OPERATE in demanding conditions, with unprecedented scrutiny of Islamic Religion, our profession, and constant developments in regulation, law and community demands.

OUR REPUTATION is created by the way the people within our firm respond to these conditions, and how they act with Muslims Ummah with colleagues, and with their communities.

OUR VALUES AND OUR CODE OF CONDUCT guide and unite us on a day-to-day basis; they help ISC partners and professionals to know what we expect of them, and what they can expect from THE ISLAMIC SITES CONSERVATION - TANZANIA.

Good governance is at the heart of what we stand for; we recognize we should always be accountable for our actions, and must maintain our independence and objectivity.

We are also committed to promoting and embracing constructive reform, and to helping Muslims follow good practice of conserving our Islamic Sites and Islamic History, while increasing value for all stakeholders.

Our values create a sense of shared identity within the Islamic Sites Conservation organization. They define what we stand for, and how we do things; they help us to work together in the most effective and fulfilling way.

We lead by example at all levels acting in a way that, exemplifies what we expect of each other and our member firms’ stake holders, as well as our community.

We work together bringing out the best in each other, and creating strong and successful working relationships. We respect the individual respecting people for who they are, and for their knowledge, skills, and experience as individuals and team members.

We seek the facts and provide insight challenging assumptions, pursuing facts, and strengthening our reputation as trusted and objective Islamic Sites Conservation advisers.

We are open and honest in our communication, sharing information, insight, and advice frequently, constructively and managing tough situations with courage and candour.

We are committed to our communities acting as responsible Muslims and broadening our skills, experience, and perspectives through work in our communities.

Above all, we act with integrity constantly striving to uphold the highest professional standards, provide sound advice, and rigorously maintain our independence with Qur’an and Sunnah guidance.

Our values are at the heart of our Code of Conduct, which defines the standards of ethical conduct in Islamic perspective, we require of people in ISC firm.

REGARDS

PARAMOUNT CHIEF MUSSA A.S. BWAKILA LUKWELE IV,
